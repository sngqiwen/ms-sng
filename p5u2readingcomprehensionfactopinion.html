  1. The first line orientates the reader to the when, where and character involved in the story.
  2. I wonder why the writer says that the dog’s future seemed dark. What does it mean and what happened to the dog?
  3. Oh, it is an abandoned dog. I think it might not be able to survive without food.
  4. What does “desperate” mean? I think it really needs food as the story says that it grew weaker day by day. If I do not eat for a day, I will feel very hungry. This dog had not eaten for DAYS!

The Extraordinary Jasmine

In 2003, inside a dark garden shed in England, a dog whimpered. Its future seemed dark. It was an abandoned greyhound which was desperate for food. Day by day, it grew weaker.

Luckily, the dog was discovered by some police officers before it was too late. They took the greyhound to a wildlife sanctuary, which was founded and run by Geoff Baines to care for sick and injured animals. When Geoff first saw the greyhound, its eyes were lifeless, and its skin lay on its ribs like a thin blanket. He knew it needed immediate care and attention.

Right: Fact/Opinion → Inferences/Conclusions
Suggested answers (A)
Fact/Opinion Inferences/Conclusions
Fact: In 2003, inside a dark garden shed in England, a dog whimpered. The setting of time and place suggests that the extract is a recount of a real event.
Opinion: Its future seemed dark. The writer wants readers to feel sympathy for the dog.
Opinion: Luckily, the dog was discovered by some police officers before it was too late. The dog would not have survived if the police officers had not found it.
Fact: Day by day, it grew weaker. The dog did not have any food for a few days.

Geoff named the greyhound Jasmine. Nursing Jasmine back to health was a long journey. Jasmine had been emotionally affected due to unpleasant past interactions with humans. She would be startled by sudden movements and loud noises. Hence, Geoff and his workers had to win her trust. They made gentle movements and talked softly whenever they were around her. Fortunately, after several weeks of care, Jasmine was nursed back to health and began to trust them.

Geoff started to look for someone suitable to adopt Jasmine. However, it seemed like Jasmine had other plans. She found herself a spot in the sanctuary. It was the couch near the entrance. Every time someone brought in an animal, she would welcome them, no matter what species, by giving them a gentle welcoming lick. Eventually, she became the official greeter at the sanctuary.

Right: Fact/Opinion → Inferences/Conclusions
Suggested answers (B)
Fact or Opinion Inference Made
Fact: Geoff named the greyhound Jasmine. The greyhound was named by Geoff and its name was Jasmine. It is a female name so the dog must be a female dog.
Fact: Nursing Jasmine back to health was a long journey. Jasmine was in very poor health and it took a lot of care and a long time for the team to help her recover fully.
Opinion: Jasmine… was trusting again. Jasmine recovered and started to trust Geoff and his workers because they took very good care of her.

Fact/Opinion Inferences/Conclusions (Suggested)
Fact: A baby deer (fawn) was found lying in the open… barely breathing. The fawn was extremely weak and might have been abandoned or separated from its mother. The hikers were worried and wanted to save it.
Fact: The veterinarian concluded that the fawn could not be saved. The fawn’s condition was so serious that professionals believed it would die soon.
Opinion: …its chance of survival was slim. The situation was almost hopeless, but the hikers still decided to try their best to save the fawn.
Fact: Jasmine mothered the fawn… kept him warm… the fawn grew stronger. Jasmine’s warmth and care likely helped the fawn recover. Jasmine behaved like a protective mother figure.

The Extraordinary Jasmine

In 2003, inside a dark garden shed in England, a dog whimpered. Its future seemed dark. It was an abandoned greyhound which was desperate for food. Day by day, it grew weaker.

Luckily, the dog was discovered by some police officers before it was too late. They took the greyhound to a wildlife sanctuary, which was founded and run by Geoff Baines to care for sick and injured animals. When Geoff first saw the greyhound, its eyes were lifeless, and its skin lay on its ribs like a thin blanket. He knew it needed immediate care and attention.

Geoff named the greyhound Jasmine. Nursing Jasmine back to health was a long journey. Jasmine had been emotionally affected due to unpleasant past interactions with humans. She would be startled by sudden movements and loud noises. Hence, Geoff and his workers had to win her trust. They made gentle movements and talked softly whenever they were around her. After several weeks of care, Jasmine was nursed back to health and began to trust them.

Apart from being the greeter of the sanctuary, Jasmine took on the role of a mother for many other animals. Once, a baby deer, a fawn, was found lying in the open by some hikers. It was barely breathing. The hikers took the fawn to the veterinarian. Upon examination, the veterinarian concluded that the fawn could not be saved. However, the hikers decided to take a chance and drove to the sanctuary to seek further assistance.

To everyone’s surprise, the fawn survived, thanks to Jasmine! She mothered the fawn, snuggled with him on the couch and kept him warm. Day by day, the fawn grew stronger and one day, he was finally out of danger. Since then, the fawn, named Bramble, stuck to Jasmine like glue. He would touch Jasmine’s nose the way a baby deer would touch its mother’s nose.

Q1. From Section B, which word has the same meaning as “shocked”?

Answer: startled

Q2(a). Was it an easy task for Geoff to nurse Jasmine back to health?

Answer: No.

Q2(b). Give two reasons from the text to support your answer for Q2(a).

Possible reasons (any two):
  1. Geoff (and his workers) had to be patient to gain her trust while helping her to heal.
  2. Jasmine’s recovery process took several weeks.

Q3. From Section C, True/False + reason.

(a) “The hikers knew that Geoff could heal the fawn.” → False
Reason: The hikers did not know if Geoff could heal the fawn, but they decided to take a chance at saving it by taking it to the sanctuary.

(b) “Jasmine and the fawn formed an amazing bond.” → True
Reason: The fawn would touch Jasmine’s nose the way a baby deer would touch its mother’s nose (the fawn treated Jasmine like its mother).

Q4. Do you think Jasmine was the only reason why the fawn survived? Why/why not?

Possible answers:
• Yes: The fawn’s survival rate was low, and many said it would not survive. With Jasmine’s company and care, the fawn proved them wrong and survived.
• No: Without medical support (and help from humans), the fawn might not have survived because it was too weak.

Q5. Do you think Jasmine was an amazing friend? Support your opinion with a reason from the text.

Possible answer: Yes. Jasmine mothered the fawn during his most difficult times. She stayed with him, kept him warm, and supported him through his recovery.
